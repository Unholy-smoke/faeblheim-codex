## Summary

- The party kills Midnakesh and its ghostly servants, though one spirit escapes.
- Back in Gufragel, the party learns that Garsay is the storm giant queen and that they should show deference.
- The party visits Karl's crew, plays dice and wins money from them.
- Bogsū shows Boss the horn. Boss appraises it as pure magical fear.
- The party grinds a chunk of the horn into vodka and drinks it, producing awful visions for nearly everyone.
- The next morning, the party takes a wagon towards the storm giants, watching Karl's wagon ahead.
- They arrive in Rosh, the storm giant citadel: floating plateaus, bridges, minarets and carved stone.
- At the top of the Spire, they see a huge glyph of Taiia: the beginning of her spell, the end of the Demon War, waiting for activation.
- Rosh is bent and broken, with storm giant bones everywhere and razor leaves growing from corpses.

## Source Notes

- Really hard fight but we kill Midnakesh and his ghostly goons. One of them floats off.
- We go to sleep in the stone shack and heal up.
- Wake up in the morning and look around.
- We return to Gufragel.
- Garsay is the storm giant queen. Advises us to kowtow.
- Before bed we nip into Karl and co's room. Playing dice. We win a bunch of money from them.
- Bogsū shows Boss the horn. He appraises it as pure magical fear. We break off a chunk, grind it up, mix in vodka and do a shot each.
- All get awful trips. Their crew as bad as ours. Elia resists.
- Night time.
- In the morning we get on a wagon up to the storm giants: two days' journey. Keep an eye on the Karl wagon ahead of us.
- Arrive in Rosh. Storm giant citadel. Carved stone, minarets, spires. Spread across floating plateaus connected by bridges.
- At the very peak of the Spire, a huge 10 km glyph floating in the sky. Taiia! The beginning of her spell, the end of the Demon War, waiting for activation.
- But the city is bent and broken. Bones of storm giant everywhere. Razor leaves growing out of corpses.
